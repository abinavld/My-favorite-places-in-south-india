Coimbatore

Coimbatore is a city in the south Indian state of Tamil Nadu. To the northwest is the centuries-old, Dravidian-style Arulmigu Subramaniyaswami Temple, Marudamalai. The colourful and intricately carved Arulmigu Patteeswarar Swamy Temple lies southeast of here. The Gass Forest Museum has a vast collection of preserved animals and tree trunks in the centre. Southeast, birds and butterflies inhabit Singanallur Lake. Coimbatore is also famous for foundry and automobile industries, manufacturing textile industry equipment, spares, motor pump sets, wet grinders and various engineering goods and services. The development of Hydroelectricity from the Pykara Falls in 1930 led to a cotton boom in Coimbatore.
